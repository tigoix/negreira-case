Overview of the Scandal

Core Allegations

The Negreira Case centers on substantial payments from FC Barcelona to José María Enríquez Negreira, the former Vice-President of Spain's Technical Committee of Referees (CTA), between 2001 and 2018 [5] .

The total payments, ranging from €7.3 million to €8.4 million, were allegedly made to influence refereeing decisions in Barcelona's favor, compromising La Liga's integrity [52] .

"It is presumed by pure logic that FC Barcelona would not pay vice-president Negreira around 7 million euros since 2001 if they were not benefited by him"
— Judge Joaquín Aguirre López

Mechanism of Alleged Influence

Referee Appointments

Negreira allegedly influenced the appointment of "like-minded" referees for Barcelona matches, ensuring officials perceived as favorable to the club [116] .

Career Advancement

The CTA controlled referee promotions and international appointments, creating potential leverage over officials' careers and future earnings [116] .

Corruption Index

An alleged "corruption index" was used to evaluate referees based on non-technical criteria, allowing less qualified officials to advance [116] .

Impact Analysis

Statistical Evidence

Academic Study Findings

A 2024 study by Hernández Trillo and Zaragoza Rivera analyzed over two decades of La Liga data:

"The results reveal a significant positive relationship between the payments made by Barcelona FC and its competitive success, even after controlling for quality"

Source: CIDE Repository [36]

Harm to Other Teams

Judge Aguirre López stated that if referee assignments were manipulated, other La Liga teams from 2001-2018 must be considered injured parties [116] .

Real Madrid joined as private prosecutor

Claiming status as injured party

Integrity Damage

Spanish Refereeing Reputation

The scandal has severely compromised the perceived impartiality of Spanish refereeing, with FIFA excluding Spanish referees from the 2025 Club World Cup [92] .

Competition Fairness

The alleged systemic corruption undermines the fundamental principle of fair competition, affecting league standings, European qualification, and relegation battles [116] .
